Crew Portal Bid User Guide : Bidding overview : Bids and bid points : Bid properties
Bid properties
Bids have the following properties:
•
Bid types
•
Bid validity period
•
Bidding deadline
•
Not granted bids
•
Bid points
•
Bid ratio
Bid types
When you create bids you can choose from a number of available bid types reflecting different roster properties, for example layover, buddy bid, day-off bid.
Bid validity period
A bid can be valid for a single timeframe, for example a day, within a bid period. It can also be repeated for a number of times during a limited time period, or until further notice (UFN). Bids can be valid for:
•
all days during the bid validity period
•
a certain day of the week during the bid validity period
•
a specific date
•
a complete week
•
a range of dates.
The combination of the selected bid type and the specified time period gives the validity period for the bid.
Example 
You can submit a bid for early check-ins as a specific day of week bid that is valid for every Tuesday in April, or as a multiple-day bid that is valid for the second week in June.
Bids that are valid until further notice will be displayed in future roster periods automatically.
Bidding deadline
Each roster period has a deadline, a last date when you can submit bids that concern that roster period. The bidding deadline is the close date in the Bid window.
Not granted bids
Although all your bids are considered in the roster production, there is no guarantee that all of them will be granted.
Some bids that may not be granted are:
•
Attractive bids
The production is not covered if everyone have these bids granted.
•
Illegal bids
The bid is illegal or impossible to combine with other bids.
•
Bids with low priority
Other bid combinations are more advantageous.
•
Impossible bids
Crew Portal Bid will not warn if a bid is impossible but for several bid types you can use the filter option to display all pairings that match the bid. However, contradicting bids are not a problem. You can bid for a weekend off and a late report time on the same date, but you should use different bid points to prioritize the alternate bids.
•
Bids that generate a bad quality roster
Bad quality rosters are primarily rosters that are not robust, that is, rosters that are sensitive to irregularities.
•
Unrealistic bids
The roster becomes unrealistic if the bid is granted, for example with only one hour work every day.
