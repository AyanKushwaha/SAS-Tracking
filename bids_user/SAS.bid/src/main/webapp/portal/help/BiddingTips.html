Bidding tips
It is your responsibility, and in your best interest, to ensure that the bids you submit are realistic. Some tips to increase the chance of your bids being granted are:
•
Bid for legal assignments. For example, you can bid for a certain pairing even if the minimum rest after the pairing is not respected, but you will not be assigned that pairing. Dependent on the implementation, Crew Portal Bid can do a limited check of the legality of your bids which will help you to create legal bids.
•
If you want a longer time off during a certain period, try splitting the bid in several shorter bids. A bid for 6 consecutive days off is less likely to be granted than 3 bids for 2 days off during the same period.
•
Submit bids that match as many pairings as possible. If you put too many limitations on your bids they have less chance of being granted. Use the filter option to find out the number of pairings that match the bid.
•
Submit bids for just about everything you want. A single bid will lead to either 0% or 100% satisfaction and crew members with few bids are more likely to end up with 0% satisfaction in tight roster solutions. Do not, however, expect all your bids to be granted.
•
Use overlapping bids. If you want a pairing to an attractive city during a certain period, submit a bid for every city you think would do and place the highest bid points on your favourite city.
•
Use both low and high bid points to differentiate the priority of your bids. If you only use high bid points the bids will have equal weight as they only are compared to each other. See also Bid points (Weighted bidding).
