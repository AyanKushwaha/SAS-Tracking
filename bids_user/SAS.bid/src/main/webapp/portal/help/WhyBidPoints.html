Bid points (Weighted bidding)
When rosters are produced several aspects have to be considered, for example legality, qualifications, work coverage, stability, quality, fairness and bids.
Bids serve as input to Crew Rostering, which uses optimization to find the best roster solution. In this process two types of rules are applied:
•
rules that are not allowed to be broken, for example legality rules or roster properties that should be avoided
•
rules that add costs for different roster properties.
The cost is not necessarily a real cost, but rather a way to compare different roster solutions. The optimizer generates a number of solutions and calculates the total cost of all rosters in each solution, the lower the cost the better the solution.
To include your bids in this process the Crew Portal Bid uses bid points and bid ratio.
Bid points
When you submit bids you assign points to each bid. Crew Portal Bid calculates the bid points of a roster by summarizing the points of all bids each roster activity grants. That is, a single pairing can add points from several bids.
Crew Portal Bid uses the bid points to calculate the bid ratio of your roster. This means that your points are not compared directly to other crew members’ points. Instead, the points indicate the relative importance of your own bids. For example, assigning three bids the points 20/10/10 will give the same result as assigning the points 200/100/100.
The bid points are the key to successful bidding; use them to differentiate between your important and less important bids. Crew Rostering strives to maximize the total bid points of your roster, but this does not automatically mean that a bid with high points are granted. Remember that a single bid, especially a repeated bid, may accumulate bid points from several roster activities.Your bid points only apply to your own roster.
Bid ratio
When Crew Rostering compares different roster solutions for all crew members it uses the bid ratio. Your bid ratio is calculated in two steps.
In the first step a reference roster is produced to find out the maximum number of bid points you could have been granted. Your reference roster is allocated individually, which means that:
•
all pairings are available to you
•
you are considered as the only crew member
•
production is not considered, that is, you can have days off assigned as long as the average duty is covered.
All rules and fairness are applied as in a production roster. If bids are in conflict the roster that generates the most bid points is used.
In the second step the bid ratio is calculated as the ratio between the bid points in your production roster and your reference roster. Each crew member has an individual target value for the bid ratio and the optimizer tries to achieve at least this value. There is no guarantee that every crew member reaches his or her target value, as the optimizer searches for the best overall roster solution.
